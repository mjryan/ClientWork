BRIDGE is a comprehensive youth development program that utilizes visual art to increase self-awareness and foster an appreciation of differences while promoting community involvement and personal responsibility. BRIDGE was designed to meet the new California State Art Curriculum Content Standards for 8th grade and 9th-12th grade coursework.

In the summer before entering ninth grade, thirty students work together with professional artists, facilitators and trained high school students to create a mosaic tile project. BRIDGE staff - exploring the concepts of inclusion and exclusion, engage students in a host of activities, discussions and art making techniques aimed at preparing them to enter high school feeling connected to each other and to the larger community. The mosaic tile project they complete together reflects their discoveries during the summer session and is given as a gift to a local organization, business or city project.

During the summer, BRIDGE takes place in two three-week sessions. Each session includes 30 participants, five to ten high school mentors, four staff and one program manager. Students meet Monday through Friday from 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM.

Upon successful completion of the summer session, students are eligible to participate in the BRIDGE school year session; a leadership program that trains twenty to thirty high school students to work as mentors in the next BRIDGE summer sessions. Those selected for the summer program will receive a stipend at the end of each three-week session.

During the school year, BRIDGE takes place November through May, with students meeting on Wednesday afternoons and Saturday mornings.
