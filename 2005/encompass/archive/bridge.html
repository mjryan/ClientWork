BRIDGE is a comprehensive youth development program that utilizes visual art to increase self-awareness, foster appreciation of difference and build community, while promoting community involvement and personal responsibility among middle and high school students. BRIDGE was designed to meet the new California State Art Curriculum Content Standards for 8th grade and 9th-12th grade coursework.

In the summer before entering ninth grade, thirty entering students will work together with professional artists, facilitators and trained high school students to create a mosaic tile project. BRIDGE staff, exploring the concepts of inclusion and exclusion, will engage students in a host of activities, discussions and art making techniques aimed at preparing them to enter high school feeling connected to each other and to the larger community. The mosaic tile project that they complete together will reflect their discoveries during the summer session and will be given as a gift to a local organization, business or city project.

During the summer, BRIDGE will take place in two three-week sessions. Each session includes 30 eighth graders, five to ten high school mentors, four staff and one program manager. Students meet Monday through Friday from 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM.

Upon successful completion of the summer session, students will be eligible to participate in the BRIDGE school year session; a leadership program that trains twenty to thirty high school students to work as mentors in the BRIDGE summer sessions. Those selected for the summer session will receive a stipend at the end of each three-week session.

During the school year, BRIDGE takes place November through May, with students meeting on Wednesday afternoons and Saturday mornings.
